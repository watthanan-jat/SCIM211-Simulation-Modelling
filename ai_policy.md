# AI Policy

AI tools may be useful in SCIM211 Simulation Modelling, but they must be used responsibly. The goal of the course is for students to understand simulation concepts, build models, write code, and explain results in their own words.

## Acceptable Uses

Students may use AI tools to:

- Review general programming concepts.
- Ask for explanations of probability or simulation ideas.
- Debug small code errors after making an honest attempt.
- Improve the clarity of writing.
- Generate alternative examples for practice.
- Check whether an explanation is understandable.

## Unacceptable Uses

Students must not use AI tools to:

- Produce a full assignment, lab, or project submission without understanding it.
- Hide the use of AI when disclosure is required.
- Submit code or explanations that they cannot explain.
- Generate fake data, fake results, or misleading analysis.
- Bypass the learning objectives of the Coffee Shop Simulation Project.

## Student Responsibility

Students are responsible for everything they submit. If AI-generated suggestions are used, students must check the mathematics, code, assumptions, and interpretation carefully.

Instructors may ask students to explain their submitted work. If a student cannot explain the modelling choices, code, or results, the work may be treated as incomplete or not genuinely their own.

## Good Practice

When using AI, keep notes on what was used and why. Treat AI output as a draft or suggestion, not as an authority. The final submission should reflect the student's own understanding and judgement.
