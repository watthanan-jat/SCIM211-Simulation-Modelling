# Lab 4: Monte Carlo Profit and Promotion Decision

## Theme

Monte Carlo simulation for business decision-making.

## Scenario

The coffee shop is considering a matcha promotion. Demand, service workload, revenue, and cost are uncertain. You will estimate expected profit, bad-day probability, and a confidence interval before making a recommendation.

## Example

Review one simulated business day and observe how repeating the experiment produces a profit distribution.

## Guided Work

Define assumptions for daily demand, average spend, promotion cost, and variable cost. Keep assumptions simple and explain them clearly.

## Coding Work

Complete the TODO cells to:

- Simulate many daily profit values.
- Estimate expected profit.
- Estimate probability of a bad day.
- Calculate a confidence interval for mean profit.
- Plot the simulated profit distribution.

## Investigation

Compare baseline and matcha promotion scenarios. Decide whether the promotion should be used.

## Checkpoint Submission

Submit the notebook, report, recommendation, and AI use statement.

## Reflection Questions

1. Why is Monte Carlo useful for uncertain profit?
2. What does expected profit hide?
3. How did you define a bad day?
4. How should the confidence interval affect the decision?
5. What extra data would make the promotion decision stronger?
