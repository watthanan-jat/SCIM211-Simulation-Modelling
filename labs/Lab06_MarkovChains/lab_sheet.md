# Lab 6: Markov Chains and Customer Loyalty

## Theme

Markov chain modelling for coffee shop customer loyalty.

## Scenario

Customers may remain loyal, become occasional visitors, or stop visiting. You will model these loyalty states with a transition matrix and investigate whether a loyalty card improves retention.

## 2-Hour Timing Plan

## Instructor Demo

The instructor will show how rows of a transition matrix represent probabilities of moving from one state to another.

## Guided Task

Define states such as loyal, occasional, and inactive. Check that each transition matrix row sums to 1.

## Coding Task

Complete the notebook TODO cells to simulate one customer path and many customer paths over several weeks.

## Investigation

Modify the transition matrix to represent a loyalty-card programme. Compare the distribution of customers across states before and after the programme.

## Checkpoint Submission

Submit the notebook, report, transition matrices, comparison summary, and AI use statement.

## Reflection Questions

1. What does the Markov assumption mean in this context?
2. Why must each transition matrix row sum to 1?
3. What did the one-customer path show?
4. How did many-customer simulation differ from one path?
5. Did the loyalty card improve retention? Explain.
