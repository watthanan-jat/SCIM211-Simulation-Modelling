# Lab 3: Random Variables and Drink Service Times

## Theme

Random variable simulation for drink-dependent service times.

## Scenario

The coffee shop serves different drinks. Some drinks are quick, while others take longer. You will simulate drink orders, assign service-time distributions, and investigate how a matcha trend changes workload.

## Example

Review how to sample from an exponential distribution and how different means affect simulated service times.

## Guided Work

Create a small menu with drink probabilities, such as coffee, tea, latte, and matcha. Use random sampling to generate customer drink orders.

## Coding Work

Complete the notebook TODO cells to:

- Simulate drink orders.
- Assign service-time means by drink type.
- Generate drink-dependent service times.
- Summarise average service time by drink.
- Create a plot comparing service-time patterns.

## Investigation

Increase the probability of matcha orders to represent a matcha trend. Compare baseline and trend scenarios.

Discuss:

- How did the average service time change?
- Which drinks create the most workload?
- What could the coffee shop manager do in response?
